## Q24 First Take

**PDD Individual Research Analysis - 001**

**Report**: PDD Holdings (PDD): 2Q24 First Take - Slight revenue miss yet another profit beat while laying out investments ahead; Buy
**Date**: 26 August 2024
**Source file**: `G:/gs/PDD Holdings (PDD)_ 2Q24 First Take_ Slight revenue miss yet another profit beat while laying out investments ahead; Buy.pdf`

**Conclusion**

Goldman maintained Buy with a 12-month SOTP target price of `$184`; report price was `$139.87`, implying `31.6%` upside. The key conclusion is that the negative share-price reaction was too harsh. Revenue slightly missed very high Street expectations, but adjusted operating profit beat for the sixth consecutive quarter. The market focused on slowing online marketing growth, management's warning about future profit volatility, and no shareholder-return plan. Goldman focused on still-rapid GMV growth, strong domestic profit, and Temu traction.

**Key data**

- 2Q revenue grew `+86% yoy`, in line with Goldman but below Visible Alpha consensus of `+89%`.
- Adjusted group operating profit reached `Rmb35bn`, `+139% yoy`, above Goldman/consensus of `Rmb31bn`.
- Online marketing services revenue grew `+29% yoy`, below Goldman/consensus of `+36%/+33%`, showing growth normalization.
- Transaction commission revenue grew `+234% yoy`, above expectations, likely driven by Temu.
- Goldman estimated Temu GMV at about `$11bn` in 2Q and `>$20bn` in 1H24.
- Management guided to `Rmb10bn` of investments over the next 12 months, mostly fee reductions and merchant support.

**Investment interpretation**

This is where the PDD thesis changed from pure monetization/profit expansion to ecosystem reinvestment. Management signaled it would trade near-term profitability for merchant ROI, user conversion and high-quality growth. Goldman interpreted this as rational long-term investment, not as a demand collapse. The most important distinction is that online marketing slowed, but GMV and transaction services were still strong.

**Investment advice / reading conclusion**

At the report date, the Buy call was a contrarian call after a sharp selloff. The right question was whether lower monetization would create stronger GMV and merchant retention. If yes, the selloff created opportunity; if not, the margin reset would become a value trap.

**Risks**

Lower online marketing revenue, Alibaba/JD/Douyin competition, Temu geopolitical risk, no shareholder return, reinvestment pressure on margins, and lack of segment disclosure for Pinduoduo vs Temu.

## Merchant Support Measures

**PDD Individual Research Analysis - 003**

**Report**: PDD Holdings (PDD): Tallying recent merchant supportive measures; manageable impact while building a healthier ecosystem; Buy
**Date**: 9 September 2024
**Source file**: `G:/gs/PDD Holdings (PDD)_ Tallying recent merchant supportive measures; manageable impact while building a healthier ecosystem; Buy.pdf`

**Conclusion**

Goldman maintained Buy with a 12-month target price of `$165`; report price was `$93.30`, implying `76.8%` upside. The report's point is simple but important: investors were overestimating the earnings damage from merchant-support measures and underestimating the ecosystem benefits. The measures were real, but Goldman viewed the P&L impact as manageable and largely already reflected in estimates.

**Key merchant-support measures**

- Eligible merchant deposits were cut by `50%`, from `Rmb1,000` to `Rmb500`.
- Estimated cash outflow: `Rmb4-5bn`, but negligible P&L impact. Total merchant deposits were `Rmb18bn` at end-2Q24.
- Buy-now-pay-after-delivery service charge was cut to `0.6%` from `1.0%`.
- Estimated annual transaction-service revenue impact: `Rmb2-3bn`; no direct marketing-service revenue impact.
- PDD improved refund and after-sales mechanisms, including promotional-service-fee credits and technology-fee refunds on returned orders.
- PDD began bearing partial logistics costs for remote regions including Xinjiang, Tibet, Gansu, Ningxia and Inner Mongolia.
- Goldman estimated the announced package broadly matched PDD's guided `Rmb10bn` merchant-support investment plan.

**Investment interpretation**

This report separates accounting effect from narrative fear. The largest item, deposit refunds, affects cash but not earnings. The service-fee cut affects transaction revenue but not online marketing revenue. Strategically, PDD was trying to repair merchant relations, align with regulatory pressure against unreasonable platform fees, and support GMV growth.

**Investment advice / reading conclusion**

The report supports a Buy if the support measures improve merchant health and sustain GMV share. At `7x 2024-25E P/E` and `5x ex-cash`, Goldman believed the market was pricing a declining business. The bull case is that PDD is deliberately reducing monetization to strengthen the platform.

**Risks**

The biggest risk is duration and escalation. If support measures expand beyond `Rmb10bn`, margin pressure may be larger. Other risks include Alibaba/Tmall/JD/Douyin competition, weaker online marketing growth, Temu policy risk and limited segment disclosure.

## US De-Minimis Policy Risk

**PDD Individual Research Analysis - 004**

**Report**: PDD Holdings (PDD): New US policies targeting de-minimis exemptions; an evolving Temu business model
**Date**: 15 September 2024
**Source file**: `G:/gs/PDD Holdings (PDD)_ New US policies targeting de-minimis exemptions; an evolving Temu business model.pdf`

**Conclusion**

Goldman maintained Buy with a 12-month target price of `$165`; report price was `$94.99`, implying `73.7%` upside. The report analyzes US policy action targeting de-minimis exemptions. Goldman's conclusion: the risk is real, but PDD's market cap and SOTP target were already assigning little/no value to Temu US, and Temu was already diversifying away from direct-air full-entrusted shipments.

**Policy and data**

- US actions targeted key products, especially textile and apparel, for exclusion from de-minimis exemption.
- Documentation requirements would include tariff classification and claimant identity.
- Consumer product safety compliance would require electronic Certificates of Compliance.
- Temu app had about `290mn` MAU in August 2024 across `78` countries.
- US contribution to Temu global MAU fell from `100%` at early-2023 launch to about `18%` by August 2024.
- Semi-entrusted model reached about `25%` of Temu US GMV.
- Temu had also launched 3P local marketplace models in Europe and semi-entrusted models in more markets.
- Goldman price checks suggested Temu was about `58%` cheaper than Amazon for selected US SKUs before full policy impact.

**Investment interpretation**

The direct-air full-entrusted model is most exposed to de-minimis reform. However, semi-entrusted/local warehouse/3P models reduce that exposure, though they may reduce revenue mark-up and SKU breadth. The critical valuation point is that Goldman did not include Temu US in its SOTP target, so the policy risk was partly pre-discounted.

**Investment advice / reading conclusion**

The report supports PDD as a 1-3 year re-rating story only if investors accept policy volatility. Domestic Pinduoduo remains the valuation anchor, while Temu ex-US remains optionality. Investors should not value Temu US aggressively until tariff, customs and app/data rules become clearer.

**Risks**

Harsher tariffs, UFLPA enforcement, logistics congestion, app/data restrictions, Europe de-minimis reform, semi-entrusted working-capital burden and lower SKU depth.

## Temu Vietnam Entry Sea Read-Across

**PDD Individual Research Analysis - 005**

**Report**: Sea Ltd. (SE): Initial thoughts on Temu's Vietnam entry - Expect limited impact in near term
**Date**: 8 October 2024
**Source file**: `G:/gs/SeaLtd.(SE)_InitialthoughtsonTemu'sVietnamentry_Expectlimitedimpactinnearterm.pdf`

**Conclusion**

This is formally a Sea Ltd. report, but it is PDD-relevant because it evaluates Temu's Southeast Asia expansion. Goldman stayed Buy-rated on Sea with a `$99` target, but the Temu read-across is mixed: Vietnam and Brunei expand Temu's regional footprint, yet the near-term threat to Shopee looked limited because Temu's local value proposition was weaker than in the US.

**Key observations**

- Temu launched in Vietnam and Brunei on 7 October 2024.
- This brought Temu's Southeast Asia presence to `5` countries after entering Thailand earlier in July.
- Goldman expected limited near-term impact on Shopee.
- Temu's prices in Vietnam were less compelling vs local SKUs on Shopee/Lazada, unlike the large US discount vs Amazon.
- Localization was weak: English-only interface and no Vietnamese option at the time.
- Shipping time was longer: Temu `4-7` days vs Shopee/Lazada `1-2` days.
- Payment options were narrow: credit card only, with no cash-on-delivery, local e-wallet or consumer financing.
- Indonesia looked difficult because regulators opposed Temu's direct cross-border model; Temu might need 1P local warehousing if it entered.

**Investment interpretation for PDD**

This report is a useful reminder that Temu's international expansion is not plug-and-play. The model works best where price advantage, SKU breadth and acceptable shipping time are large enough to offset localization gaps. In Southeast Asia, local platforms already have dense merchant networks, local payments and fast delivery. That reduces Temu's immediate disruption potential.

**Investment advice / reading conclusion**

The report does not break the Temu thesis, but it lowers expectations for instant Southeast Asia success. PDD investors should evaluate Temu by region rather than applying one global growth multiple. The most important signals are localization progress, delivery speed, local payment support and whether Temu can preserve price advantage under local regulatory constraints.

**Risks**

Weak localization, higher relative prices, slower delivery, local SME protection, payment friction, and regulatory pressure against direct cross-border shipping.

## Cross-Border Expert Session

**Date**: 11 May 2025
**Source file**: `G:/gs/2025-05/China eCommerce Cross-border expert session takeaways Focusing on post-tariff pricing adjustments, supply-chain responses and Temu.PDF`

**Conclusion**

This expert-session report validates the Temu model-shift thesis after US tariff/de-minimis changes. Goldman continued to value only Temu non-US in PDD's SOTP, with a `$152` PDD target. The expert's message was balanced: tariffs narrow Temu's price advantage and semi-entrusted has operational disadvantages, but moderate tariff levels may be absorbed across the value chain and Temu still has non-US growth opportunities.

**Key takeaways**
